CPT Contemporary Political Theory Group

The CPT is organised by graduates of the University of Warwick. It is a weekly, on-line, pre-read reading group which tackles the most rigorous journal articles (including but not limited to, Ethics, Philosophy and Public Affairs, Journal of Political Philosophy and Utilitas in Political Theory in recent times (10 years).

Since 2022, we've changed into a reading group for books. The first session will be on 09/02, during which we will discuss Scanlon, Thomas. 1998. What We Owe to Each Other. Belknap Press of Harvard University Press, up to p.78

We encourage like-minded scholars to join us in this hopefully fruitful and enriching experience in understanding, debating and dissecting contemporary debates in political theory.

  1. We foresee equal chances for participants to discuss during CPT, we expect all participants to pre-read the article and come to CPT sessions prepared for a rigorous discussion.
  2. We follow a voluntary sign-up of chair, this entails duties and obligations as follows:
    1. Proposal of a suitable paper in above journals for reading and discussion, 7 days before the session takes place;
    2. To give a 15 min presentation at the beginning of CPT meetings on the main argument of the reading, as well as posing leading questions for discussions;
    3. Chair the session with a hand+finger system - while participating in the discussion.
